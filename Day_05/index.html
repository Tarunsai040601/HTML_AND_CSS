Bhagat singh

Indian freedom fighters were individuals who spearheaded, participated in, or sacrificed their lives to end British colonial rule (1857–1947). Key leaders used varied strategies, including non-violent movements (Mahatma Gandhi), armed rebellion (Bhagat Singh, Chandra Shekhar Azad), and geopolitical maneuvering (Subhas Chandra Bose), with significant contributions from leaders like Sardar Patel, Rani Lakshmibai, and Jawaharlal Nehru.
